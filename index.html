Christian Abou Mrad

I am a PhD student in Artificial Intelligence and Robotics at Oregon State University, advised by Dr. Houssam Abbas. I’m generally interested in ways to enable autonomous systems to be safe and reliable in complex, dynamic environments.

My current research develops geometric representations of Temporal Logic formulas, which make it easier to sample, project, and interpret system behaviors. This approach supports new methods for verification, trajectory planning, and control synthesis for Cyber-Physical Systems.

Previously, I earned a bachelor's degree in Computer and Communication Engineering from The American University of Beirut, Lebanon, where I built a strong foundation in software, hardware systems, and signal processing.


Research

Research I 2023-202_

Geometric Representations of Signal Temporal Logic for Verification and Control

Signal Temporal Logic (STL) lets us express requirements like “always avoid obstacles” or “reach a target within 10 seconds,” but robots can’t directly use this language. My current research explores how to translate STL formulas into geometry: a space of points that represent all possible behaviors satisfying the requirements. Early results show this geometric view can guide falsification (finding unsafe behaviors), improve trajectory planning, and help design controllers that make autonomous systems, like UAVs, safer and more reliable. This is ongoing work, and we are refining these methods with the goal of making them practical for real-world autonomous systems.

Research II 2025-202_

Exploring Control and Navigation with Crazyflie Drones

Alongside my main research, I work with the Crazyflie nano drones as a platform for testing control models and experimenting with navigation strategies. This work is still at an early stage and mostly exploratory — getting comfortable with the hardware, running small experiments, and testing different control approaches. Over time, these experiments will develop into larger projects on safe navigation and multi-robot systems.
